## The decisions

### The daily-cleaner queue is a trust signal, not just a backlog — *model-behavior-as-design*

Six open PRs from daily-cleaner (#155–#165) represent validated, Opus-confidence-checked stale-doc edits that are sitting idle. The pipeline works as follows: daily-cleaner opens a PR against daily-logger, runs it through Claude Opus with a structured prompt that scores each proposed edit as high, medium, or low confidence, and filters out anything below medium before the PR is opened. "High confidence" means the model assessed no ambiguity about correctness and no risk of semantic change; "medium" means the edit is directionally correct but a human spot-check is warranted. "Low confidence" edits are dropped at generation time — they never become PRs. The human review gate exists because even medium-confidence edits touch prose that carries intentional meaning, and a model can't reliably distinguish stylistic choice from unintentional drift. That rationale is sound. What is not sound is letting six of these pile up. The longer these go unreviewed, the more the cleaner's output is implicitly treated as noise — which undermines the entire point of having a confidence-gated pipeline. One open question this surfaces: should high-confidence-only PRs with no human changes since the last merge be eligible for auto-merge? That question is worth a formal ADR, not just an investigation ticket — the article body has already assembled the argument. The case for auto-merge on high-confidence PRs is strong; the case against it should be stated explicitly or the gate should be removed.
