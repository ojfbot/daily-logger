Today is the first day back after a two-week intentional pause — one week of vacation, one week absorbed by other work. The pause was deliberate: the question was how the autonomous fleet would behave with no human in the loop. The answer is more interesting than "it worked" or "it broke." It worked for eight days, then it broke on a billing boundary, and nothing in the system surfaced the failure. This entry is the digest of that window — what the pipeline managed on its own, where it stalled, and the structural lessons that aren't visible from inside any single article.

## What shipped during the absence

Nothing human-authored. Across the 14-day window (2026-04-13 → 2026-04-26) the only commits across the entire fleet were automated:

- **Daily blog (2026-04-14 → 2026-04-21)** — the ojfbot-blog[bot] pipeline published 8 consecutive draft articles, each correctly summarising single-commit days. The articles themselves audited the silence in real time, naming the daily-cleaner queue depth and Gas Town gate as compounding drag. Every article was Opus-generated, marked `status: "draft"`, and never reviewed.
- **Daily cleaner (2026-04-14 → 2026-04-21)** — the cleaner bot opened 8 PRs against `daily-logger` (#155, #157, #159, #161, #163, #165, #167, #169), each high/medium-confidence stale-doc patches with green CI. None merged. The queue is now stacked one deep per day with the oldest 13 days old.

That is the entire shipping inventory. Eight blog drafts and eight cleaner PRs, all autonomous, all unreviewed.

## What broke

### The auto-blog pipeline failed silently on a depleted Anthropic API credit balance — *ci-cd*

On 2026-04-22 the Daily Dev Blog GitHub Action started failing. The error from the most recent run (2026-04-27 11:09 UTC):

> `invalid_request_error: Your credit balance is too low to access the Anthropic API. Please go to Plans & Billing to upgrade or purchase credits.`

Five consecutive runs failed with the same error: 2026-04-23, 2026-04-24, 2026-04-25, 2026-04-26, 2026-04-27. Each run consumed the workflow's full pre-API setup time before erroring out — a non-trivial cost in failed CI minutes for zero output. No alert fired. No issue was opened. The article gap was only visible by listing `_articles/` and noticing no entries past 2026-04-21.

The Daily Cleaner workflow on the same repo continued succeeding throughout — different prompt size, different token economics, presumably stayed under the credit threshold. So the cleaner kept queueing PRs into a repo whose own blog pipeline was failing daily, which is a useful detail: the two pipelines are coupled by neither alerting nor budget governance.

### Three Claude session beads from 2026-04-11/12 never closed — *fleet-hardening*

`bead-emit active-sessions` returns four sessions today. Three of them are stale by 15+ days: `core-agent-worker` started 2026-04-12, `hq-session-472dc62d` started 2026-04-11, `hq-session-10fbaaee` started 2026-04-11. The bead store has no auto-cleanup for sessions that exceeded a sensible lifetime, so the active-session list now overstates concurrency on every standup. This was invisible until the standup ran today and showed 4 active sessions when only one is real.

### Local clone drift made the gap look longer than it was — *tooling-for-iteration*

Initial reconnaissance off the local clone reported a 14-day blackout (2026-04-14 → 2026-04-27 with no articles). Pulling `origin/main` revealed 8 articles already published — the local working copy was 9 commits behind on `daily-logger`, 2 behind on `shell` and `lean-canvas`, 1 behind on `mrplug`. The "blackout" was 5 days, not 14. This matters for forensics: when the diagnostic instrument disagrees with the system of record, the instrument's verdict has to be checked against the remote first or every claim downstream is wrong.

## What the auto-articles already noticed (and was right about)

The 2026-04-19 article ("Saturday consolidation") flagged that the daily-cleaner queue had crossed from "minor housekeeping backlog" into "queue problem." The 2026-04-20 article ("Sunday hold") sharpened it: "should high-confidence-only PRs with no human changes since the last merge be eligible for auto-merge?" — and answered its own question by noting that the queue had already implicitly answered yes. The 2026-04-21 article ("Monday's single commit") named the structural threshold directly: a queue this deep is a discipline gap, not a tooling gap.

The articles were right. The cleaner queue is now 8 deep and the oldest entry (#155) has been mergeable since 2026-04-14. The pattern they predicted held. What they didn't and couldn't predict: that the pipeline writing them would itself stop the next day on a billing failure that no part of the system was watching.

## The structural lesson

The fleet ran headless for two weeks and produced exactly the right diagnostic of its own state right up until the moment the diagnostic stopped. That is a good outcome for the article-generation pipeline and a bad outcome for the alerting pipeline. There is no equivalent of the daily-cleaner for failed workflows themselves — no bot watching the bots — and the only reason the credit-depletion failure surfaced today is that the human came back. The pipeline's own observability ends where the pipeline ends.

The fix isn't more articles. The fix is a small workflow that watches workflow outcomes, fires an issue or a notification when daily-blog fails N times in a row, and (separately) writes a budget-exhaustion check ahead of API calls so the failure mode is "skip with a logged reason" rather than "fail with a CI minute burned." Both of those are smaller than they sound and considerably smaller than building another article generator.

## What's next

**Refill Anthropic API credits and verify the daily-blog pipeline recovers.** The fix is upstream of any code change — confirm the workflow runs cleanly tomorrow morning before treating the gap as closed.

**Decide the daily-cleaner queue.** Eight green PRs is a discipline call, not a code call. Either merge them in date order (a 30-minute scan-and-merge session) or write the auto-merge ADR the 2026-04-20 article assembled the argument for. Both close the loop; standing pat does not.

**Run `/validate` on shell's `/api/beads` filesystem fallback.** This is the same single gate the auto-articles named for the past 9 days. It hasn't moved because nothing has been moving. It is the first thing to move.

**Triage the action board.** Sixteen actions, oldest 23 days. Anything older than 14 days without commit activity gets closed or rescoped. The action board is supposed to be a clarity source.

**Write the workflow-failure alert.** This is the new tech-debt item. The credit-depletion failure was invisible for five days because nothing was watching. Whatever the smallest version of "fail loudly" looks like, build that.
